README

// what this is, how it works, and who to blame

HOW TO USE

> pick a city from the dropdown up top.

> the map is a Google My Maps embed. the pins are the spots — tap one, get the location, go skate it.

> no login. no install. no cookie banner asking about your feelings.

WHY IT LOOKS LIKE 1983

> because the alternative is one more clean white app with rounded corners begging for a tap.

> the map underneath is plain Google — we can't rip that out yet. everything around it is deliberate: green phosphor, sharp edges, a terminal you'd find in a squat wired to a bulletin board of spots.

> call it time travel. back to when a spot was something you got told about, not something an algorithm served you.

CREDITS

> built and kept alive by the locals. no company, no brand, no roadmap deck.

> spot data lives in Google My Maps; the pins come from whoever bothered to add them. supported, allegedly, by ROLLTROTTEL-VERBAND.

DISCLAIMER

> skate at your own risk. the concrete does not care about you, and the bollards care even less.

> spots may be private property, long gone, or actively hostile. scope it before you commit, and don't torch what the locals built.

> respect the locals. respect the pedestrians. leave the spot better than you found it.
